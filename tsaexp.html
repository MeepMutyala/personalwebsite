My TSA Experience

My Experiences in TSA

Joining TSA in the 7th grade, I didn’t know much of what I was getting into. I competed in a lot of clubs at the time, including BPA, Math League, and Science Olympiad. I valued TSA as just another club. Until the State Conference.

Seeing the amount of people there and their dedication to TSA inspired me. The amount of effort other put in their events amazed me, and I decided that TSA was something I wanted to continue, and to work harder in.

Although I didn’t place in any of my events that day, I worked harder the following year. I was elected as my chapter’s President in 8th grade, practiced and worked on my events rigorously, and placed in two of my events, what seemed to be a tremendous accomplishment to me.

When I entered freshman year of high school, I attended my chapter’s TSA interest meeting. The meeting was led by a state officer who attends our school, whose name is Tyler Rouwhorst. I met him at the state conference the previous year, when he talked to me to win my vote over. Little did I know, that Tyler would later inspire me to run for state office too.

After considering it for a while, I decided to run for state office.

Over the next few months I worked hard on a few events, but primarily on my campaign and at the state conference. I was elected as the Vice-President of Delaware TSA. This was an achievement I am certainly proud of, and it marked only the beginning of my future in TSA.
